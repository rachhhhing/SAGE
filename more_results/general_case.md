**Table 1. Example of extracted model specifications used in the modeling strategy transfer evaluation.**

| Question     | There are two coal mines, A and B, with monthly coal supplies of at least 80 tons and 100 tons, respectively. They are responsible for supplying coal to three residential areas, which require 55 tons, 75 tons, and 50 tons of coal per month, respectively. The distances between mine A and these three residential areas are 10 kilometers, 5 kilometers, and 6 kilometers. The distances between mine B and these three residential areas are 4 kilometers, 8 kilometers, and 15 kilometers. How should these two coal mines distribute the coal to the three residential areas to minimize the ton-kilometers of transportation? |
| ------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **SIRL**     | **Sets:** Mines: {A, B}; Residential areas: {1, 2, 3}. **Parameters:** Supply of coal from mine i: supply_A = 80, supply_B = 100. Demand of coal at residential area j: demand_1 = 55, demand_2 = 75, demand_3 = 50. Distance from mine i to residential area j: distance_A,1 = 10, distance_A,2 = 5, distance_A,3 = 6, distance_B,1 = 4, distance_B,2 = 8, distance_B,3 = 15. **Decision Variables:** x_i,j: tons of coal transported from mine i to residential area j, where i in {A, B} and j in {1, 2, 3}. **Objective Function:** Minimize sum of distance_i,j * x_i,j over i in {A, B}, j in {1, 2, 3}. **Constraints:** Supply limit for each mine: x_A,1 + x_A,2 + x_A,3 <= 80; x_B,1 + x_B,2 + x_B,3 <= 100. Demand at each residential area: x_A,1 + x_B,1 >= 55; x_A,2 + x_B,2 >= 75; x_A,3 + x_B,3 >= 50. |
| **StepORLM** | **Sets and Parameters:** Mines: A, B. Residential Areas: 1, 2, 3. Supply capacities: Mine A: 80 tons; Mine B: 100 tons. Demand requirements: Residential Area 1: 55 tons; Residential Area 2: 75 tons; Residential Area 3: 50 tons. Distances (in kilometers): From Mine A to Residential Area 1: 10 km; From Mine A to Residential Area 2: 5 km; From Mine A to Residential Area 3: 6 km; From Mine B to Residential Area 1: 4 km; From Mine B to Residential Area 2: 8 km; From Mine B to Residential Area 3: 15 km. **Decision Variables:** x_A1: Tons of coal transported from Mine A to Residential Area 1; x_A2: Tons of coal transported from Mine A to Residential Area 2; x_A3: Tons of coal transported from Mine A to Residential Area 3; x_B1: Tons of coal transported from Mine B to Residential Area 1; x_B2: Tons of coal transported from Mine B to Residential Area 2; x_B3: Tons of coal transported from Mine B to Residential Area 3. **Objective Function:** Minimize the total ton-kilometers of transportation: 10*x_A1 + 5*x_A2 + 6*x_A3 + 4*x_B1 + 8*x_B2 + 15*x_B3. **Constraints:** Supply constraints: x_A1 + x_A2 + x_A3 <= 80; x_B1 + x_B2 + x_B3 <= 100. Demand constraints: x_A1 + x_B1 = 55; x_A2 + x_B2 = 75; x_A3 + x_B3 = 50. |
| **SAGE**     | **Sets:** Mines: A, B. Residential areas: 1, 2, 3. **Parameters:** Supply: mine_A >= 80, mine_B >= 100. Demand: area_1 = 55, area_2 = 75, area_3 = 50. Distances (km): A->1: 10, A->2: 5, A->3: 6, B->1: 4, B->2: 8, B->3: 15. **Decision Variables:** x_ij: tons transported from mine i to area j (continuous, non-negative). **Objective:** Minimize total ton-kilometers = 10*x_A1 + 5*x_A2 + 6*x_A3 + 4*x_B1 + 8*x_B2 + 15*x_B3. **Constraints:** Supply constraints: x_A1 + x_A2 + x_A3 >= 80; x_B1 + x_B2 + x_B3 >= 100. Demand constraints: x_A1 + x_B1 = 55; x_A2 + x_B2 = 75; x_A3 + x_B3 = 50. |
